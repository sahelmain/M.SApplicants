IELTS-GPA Relationship by Country

This model examines how an applicant's IELTS score relates to their GPA and country of citizenship.

How Calculated (Technical Details)
We used linear regression to predict IELTS-equivalent scores. This means we fit a mathematical model to see how much GPA (including a curve for GPA²) and country affect IELTS scores, holding the other constant.
IELTS_EQ = β₀ + β₁ × GPA + β₂ × GPA² + γc × Country + ε
(β₀: Baseline, β₁: GPA effect, β₂: GPA curve, γc: Country effect, ε: Error)

Applicant Quality Distribution

This identifies the bottom 10% of applicants based on a combined "quality" score derived from their standardized GPA and IELTS scores.

How Calculated (Technical Details)
We used Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to create the single quality score. PCA is a technique to combine related numbers into one summary score.
Quality Score = w₁ × zGPA + w₂ × zIELTS (weights from PCA)
The "bottom 10%" are those with the lowest combined scores (below the 10th percentile threshold).
Threshold = 10th percentile of all Quality Scores
